Sunflowers at Spring Gardens
============================

Sunflowers are the giants of Spring Gardens. Maria Alvarez plants them
in the Front Walk every June, where they replace the spent tulips and
provide a wall of yellow until late September.

Featured varieties
------------------

- Russian Mammoth — the classic yellow giant, up to 3.5 metres tall.
- Velvet Queen   — a deep crimson-red sunflower for the Heritage
                   Border.
- Lemon Queen    — a soft pale-yellow multi-headed variety.
- Teddy Bear     — a fluffy dwarf sunflower (60 cm) for the front of
                   the border.

See catalog.json for the full list, varieties.csv for a quick table,
and garden_plan.yaml for where each sunflower lives.

Care notes
----------

Sunflowers are the easiest crop in Spring Gardens. Maria's routine is:

  1. Sow seeds direct in late May once the soil is warm.
  2. Water deeply once a week — sunflowers are drought tolerant once
     established.
  3. Stake the tall varieties (Russian Mammoth, Velvet Queen) once
     they reach 1 metre.

Companion planting
------------------

Sunflowers are kept on the opposite side of the garden from the roses
because they shade them out. They are interplanted with squash and
sweetcorn (a "three sisters" planting) and bordered with daisies.
