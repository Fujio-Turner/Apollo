# Roses at Spring Gardens

Roses are the centerpiece of **Spring Gardens**. Our head gardener,
**Maria Alvarez**, maintains three beds dedicated to roses: the South
Wall climbers, the Centre Court bushes, and the Heritage Border.

## Featured varieties

- **Crimson Glory** — a deep-red hybrid tea, blooming May–October.
- **Iceberg** — a pure-white floribunda that flowers continuously.
- **New Dawn** — a soft-pink climber trained along the South Wall.
- **Lady of Shalott** — a coral English shrub rose by David Austin.

See the [catalog](catalog.json) for the full list, the
[varieties table](varieties.csv) for a quick comparison, and the
[2026 garden plan](garden_plan.yaml) for where each rose lives.

## Care notes

Roses share the same watering schedule as the [tulips](tulips.md) in
spring, but they need much heavier feeding through summer. Maria
follows the routine described in the general
[Care Guide](care_guide.md), with the rose-specific additions below:

1. Apply a balanced rose feed every six weeks from April to August.
2. Deadhead spent blooms weekly to encourage reflowering.
3. Prune hard in February — about a third of the previous year's
   growth.

## Companion planting

Maria interplants every rose bed with garlic and chives — the strong
scent deters aphids. Daisies line every rose bed as a low border;
sunflowers are kept on the opposite side of the garden because they
shade out roses.
