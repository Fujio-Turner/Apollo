# Tulips at Spring Gardens

Tulips open the season at **Spring Gardens**. **Maria Alvarez** plants
roughly 4,000 tulip bulbs every November in the Front Walk and the
Heritage Border — the same border that holds our heritage roses.

## Featured varieties

- **Queen of Night** — almost-black, late-flowering single tulip.
- **Apricot Beauty** — a soft-orange single early tulip.
- **Princess Irene** — a fragrant orange-and-purple triumph tulip.
- **Spring Green** — an ivory and green viridiflora tulip.

The full list lives in the [catalog](catalog.json), summarized in the
[varieties table](varieties.csv), and mapped in the
[2026 garden plan](garden_plan.yaml).

## Care notes

Tulips are easy compared with [roses](roses.md). Maria's routine,
echoed in the general [Care Guide](care_guide.md), is:

1. Plant bulbs 15 cm deep in November in well-drained soil.
2. Water once at planting and again only if the spring is dry.
3. Lift and store bulbs after the foliage yellows in June.

## Companion planting

Tulips are interplanted with daffodils for a longer display. Daisies
self-seed throughout the Heritage Border and bloom right after the
tulips fade, so the bed is rarely empty. Sunflowers replace the
tulips in the Front Walk for the summer.
